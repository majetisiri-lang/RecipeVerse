The Phase 1 mindset: Build only what is absolutely necessary to answer one question — "Will food creators share recipes here and will other people come back to read them?" Everything else waits. Resist scope creep with everything you have.
📋
Features — what gets built in Phase 1
  • User sign up & login Must
    The foundation. Without accounts there is no community, no feed, no follows. Google OAuth + email. Powered by Clerk — takes 1 day to build.
  • User profiles Must
    Every creator needs a public page showing their name, bio, avatar, and recipe list. This is their identity on the platform.
  • Recipe creation — typed form Must
    The baseline creation method. Title, ingredients, steps, cook time, servings. Every other creation method (voice, import) is built on top of this form.
  • Voice-to-recipe AI Must
    RecipeVerse's signature differentiator. Whisper transcribes audio, Claude structures it. No other platform has this. It must be in Phase 1 — it's the reason people will talk about the app.
  • One-click social import (Instagram, YouTube, Facebook) Must
    Lowers the barrier for creators who already have content. Paste a URL and AI extracts the recipe. Without this, asking creators to retype everything is a dealbreaker.
  • Photo & video upload Must
    Food is visual. Recipes without photos get almost no engagement. Cover photo + per-step photos. Powered by Uploadthing.
  • Home feed Must
    The daily habit screen. Shows recipes from followed creators. New users see trending recipes so the feed is never empty. This is what makes users return.
  • Explore / discover page Must
    How new users discover content before they follow anyone. Trending, new this week, browse by cuisine. The growth engine — every shared recipe leads here.
  • Follow / unfollow Must
    What transforms this from a recipe website into a social platform. Without follows there is no personalised feed and no creator-audience relationship.
  • Likes & saves to collections Must
    Likes give creators instant feedback. Saves give users a personal recipe box. Both are the most frequent social actions and take one day to build.
  • Comments & star ratings Must
    Comments build community — "I made this and it was amazing!" keeps creators motivated. Ratings help users trust recipes before cooking them.
  • Search with filters Must
    Users arrive with intent — "I want a quick vegetarian pasta." Without search they leave. PostgreSQL full-text covers MVP needs at zero extra cost.
  • In-app notifications Important
    Brings users back. "Ji-Yeon liked your recipe" is the loop that creates habit. Without notifications, retention collapses after week 1.
  • Content reporting / flagging Important
    Every platform needs safety tools from day one. One spam recipe can damage trust with all users. Cheap to build, expensive not to have.

Risks — what could go wrong and how to handle it
  • High
    Platform launches with no content ("empty restaurant problem")
    Mitigation: Recruit 50–100 food creators before launch day. Pre-seed 500+ quality recipes. Never show new users an empty feed — always fall back to trending.
  • High
    Voice AI misinterprets accents or unusual ingredient names
    Mitigation: Always show a full editable review screen. Highlight low-confidence fields in yellow. Provide a "type instead" fallback at every step. Monitor user edit rates to improve the prompt.
  • Medium
    Social import blocked by Instagram/Facebook API restrictions
    Mitigation: Use third-party scraping services (Apify) as the primary method. Build a fallback where users can paste the caption text manually if the URL fails.
  • Medium
    8-week timeline slips due to scope creep
    Mitigation: Ruthlessly cut anything not in the confirmed feature list. A shipped MVP with 12 features beats an unfinished one with 20. Assign a designated "scope guardian" — someone who says no.
  • Low
    Supabase free tier pauses the project (inactivity after 1 week)
    Mitigation: Set up a simple ping/cron job to keep the DB active. Upgrade to Supabase Pro ($25/mo) once the MVP launches.

Features — what gets built in Phase 2
  • Native mobile app (iOS + Android via Expo) Must
    Food apps belong on mobile. Most cooking happens in the kitchen, away from a laptop. Once the web product is validated, native app is the biggest retention lever available. Expo shares code with the web app.
  • Push notifications Must
    Requires the native app first. Push notifications bring users back when they're not already in the app — "Marco just posted a new recipe" is far more powerful than an in-app alert.
  • Activity feed (following creators' new posts) Must
    Currently the feed is chronological. Phase 2 adds a real-time activity stream — see when followed creators post, like, and comment. Makes the platform feel alive.
  • Video reels / short cooking videos Must
    Food content lives on video. TikTok and Reels proved this. Short cooking videos with an auto-attached recipe card is RecipeVerse's unique format — you watch the reel, the full recipe is one tap away.
  • "Made this!" feature with user photos Must
    The most powerful social proof on a recipe platform. When users post photos of dishes they cooked, it shows the recipe works and motivates others to try it. Also keeps creators engaged — nothing motivates posting more than seeing fans cook your food.
  • Digital cookbooks / collections (shareable) Important
    Creators can organise recipes into themed cookbooks ("My 30-Minute Meals", "Christmas Recipes") and share them as a link. Each shared cookbook is a growth channel — every click is a potential new user.
  • Creator analytics dashboard Important
    Creators who can see their growth (views, saves, follower growth, top recipes) are 3x more likely to keep posting. Simple stats: weekly views, saves, new followers. Not complex — just useful.
  • Real-time voice transcription (shows words as you speak) Important
    Massive UX improvement to the voice feature. Instead of recording blindly, users see their words appear in real time. Increases trust and reduces re-records. Requires Deepgram or Google Speech streaming API.
  • AI ingredient substitutions Important
    "I don't have X — what can I use instead?" One of the most searched food questions on the internet. Having AI answer it inside RecipeVerse keeps users on the platform instead of going to Google.
  • Multi-language support (Spanish, French, Arabic) Important
    After launch analytics will show where users are coming from. Localise the top 3 languages by user base. Whisper already handles 50+ languages for voice — the UI is the remaining work.
  • Cooking mode (full-screen step view, keep screen awake) Important
    The recipe detail page needs a dedicated "I'm cooking now" mode — large text, ingredient checkboxes, screen stays on, no distractions. This turns RecipeVerse into a kitchen companion, not just a website.
  • Upgrade search to Algolia Important
    Once the recipe library grows past 10,000 recipes, PostgreSQL full-text search starts showing its limits. Algolia adds typo-tolerance, instant results, and smarter relevance. Only migrate when users complain about search quality.

    Creators churn before analytics and growth tools exist
    Mitigation: Personally message every active creator at 30 days. Ask what they need. Even a basic spreadsheet showing their stats builds loyalty until the dashboard is built.

    Algolia costs exceed budget after migration
    Mitigation: Only migrate when PostgreSQL search is clearly failing users. Algolia free tier covers 10k records — plenty for Phase 2 launch.

The Phase 3 mindset: RecipeVerse earns money when creators earn money. The platform takes a small cut of creator revenue — this aligns success. Never introduce monetization until creators have an audience worth monetizing. A creator with 500 followers cannot sell subscriptions. One with 5,000 can.
💰
Features — monetization and scale
  • Creator tips (one-tap payments) Must
    The simplest monetization feature. After cooking a recipe, users can send the creator a $1–$10 tip. RecipeVerse takes 10%. Low friction, no subscription required. This is the feature that makes creators start treating RecipeVerse as a real income source.
  • Paid recipes (one-time purchase) Must
    Creators can lock a recipe behind a small paywall ($1.99–$4.99). "My secret jerk chicken recipe — $2.99." RecipeVerse takes 10%. Creates a direct market for signature recipes and gives creators a reason to keep their best content on the platform.
  • Creator subscriptions (monthly access) Must
    Creators offer a monthly subscription ($2.99–$9.99) for exclusive recipes, behind-the-scenes content, and early access to new posts. RecipeVerse takes 15%. The most sustainable revenue model — recurring income for creators and platform alike.
  • RecipeVerse Pro (user subscription) Must
    Optional $4.99/month plan for power users — unlimited collections, no ads (when ads launch), advanced filters, cooking mode enhancements. Secondary revenue that doesn't compete with creator earnings.
  • Meal planner with shopping list Must
    Plan meals for the week from saved recipes. Auto-generate a shopping list. One of the most-requested features on any food platform. Drives daily active use — users open the app to check their meal plan, not just browse.
  • Live cooking sessions Important
    Creators go live while cooking. Viewers follow along in real time, ask questions, and tip during the session. High engagement and strong community signal. Requires WebRTC streaming infrastructure — significant engineering effort.
  • Brand marketplace (creator × food brands) Important
    Food brands post paid collaboration briefs. Creators apply. RecipeVerse facilitates the deal and takes 20%. Needs 50,000+ MAU to attract meaningful brand interest. KitchenAid, Heinz, and Oatly all spend heavily on food creator partnerships.
  • AI meal suggestions ("what can I cook with these ingredients?") Important
    User enters what's in their fridge. AI suggests recipes from the RecipeVerse library that match. Keeps users on the platform instead of going to Google. Drives saves and follows of creators whose recipes are suggested.
  • Verified creator program + badges Important
    Notable creators get a checkmark and additional tools (priority support, featured placement, early monetization access). Creates aspiration — every creator wants to be verified. Drives posting volume.
  • SEO-optimised recipe pages + sitemap Must
    By Phase 3 you have thousands of recipes. Each one is a potential Google search result. Proper schema.org/Recipe markup, fast server-side rendering, and automatic sitemaps turn RecipeVerse into a free organic traffic machine.
  • Infrastructure: Redis cache + background jobs + CDN for video Important
    At 100k users, the feed must be cached (Redis), heavy tasks (email, notifications, AI processing) must run in background queues (Inngest), and videos must be served from a CDN. These are not features — they're the engineering upgrades that keep the platform fast at scale.

Risks
  • High
    Introducing monetization before creators have an audience
    Mitigation: Hard gate — only unlock monetization for creators with 500+ followers. A creator with 50 followers earning $0 is demoralising. One with 2,000 followers earning $50/month is a success story that markets the platform.
  • High
    Payment processing fraud or chargebacks
    Mitigation: Use Stripe Connect — it handles fraud detection, chargebacks, and compliance. Never build payment processing yourself. Add a 7-day hold on creator payouts before releasing funds.
  • Medium
    Live streaming infrastructure is complex and expensive to build
    Mitigation: Use a managed service like Mux or Livekit instead of building WebRTC from scratch. Budget $500–2,000/month for streaming infrastructure. Gate live behind a Pro subscription.
  • Medium
    Brand marketplace requires significant sales effort to attract brands
    Mitigation: Don't build the marketplace until you have 50k+ users. Then start with 5 direct brand partnerships manually before automating. Prove the concept with one KitchenAid campaign before building a full marketplace.
  • Medium
    Platform performance degrades at scale (slow feed, slow search)
    Mitigation: Add Redis caching for the feed before hitting 50k users, not after. Performance issues at scale are much harder to fix than preventing them. Budget 20% of Phase 3 engineering time for infrastructure work.
  • Low
    Tax and legal complexity for international creator payments
    Mitigation: Stripe handles most of this automatically. Consult a startup-focused accountant before launching paid features. Budget $2,000–5,000 for legal setup once — not ongoing.

The Phase 3 north star: A creator with 2,000 followers on RecipeVerse earns at least $50/month from tips and paid recipes. When that story exists, it markets itself. Every food creator with an Instagram account will hear about it and want to join.
The one rule that overrides everything
Never start the next phase until the current one is working.
Phase 2 features built on a broken Phase 1 just accelerate the failure. Phase 3 monetization on a platform with no engaged users is burning money. The metrics gates exist for a reason — treat them as real gates, not suggestions. The best startups are disciplined about what they don't build.
